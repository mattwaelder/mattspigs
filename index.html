Guinea Pigs, for the uninitiated are vegetable compactors which lack traditional hydraulic mechanisms as well as tails. Guinea pigs are not from papua new guinea and are from a cage in my livingroom. Caring for Guinea Pigs

Space Considerations

Guinea pigs should always exist in pairs, at least. This is because piggies are social creatures and get a lot of comfort from a cagemate. Because of this, and the fact that piggies are not the best climbers, they require a sizeable living space. There are lots of numbers out there, but for two guinea pigs you should try to have at least 8 square feet. This isnt a hard rule, however, and a lot can be said about open space versus space taken up by toys and hideys. In general, the bigger the space, the happier the herd.

how much food??

Guinea pigs eat a LOT... like, a lot. I go to Vons like twice a week to stock up on veggies for them piggies. You want to give them things like romaine lettuce, and green-leaf lettuce rather than those heads of lettuce that have no nutritional value. They should also have a good amount of vitamin C in their diet since they cant produce it themselves--think bell peppers. Avoid foods like spinach and kale, the dark greens are bad for pig health over time but are fine in moderation. Other than the veggies, pigs ought to be fed BALES OF HAY. Yea, like a horse would eat. Timothy hay in unholy quantities needs to be pumped into the pigs on the daily. For an OPTIMAL PIG, something like 70% of its diet should be hay. There's also the pellets, theyre kind of expensive but they give the aforementioned vitamin C, and the pigs like \'em. If those pigs been good you can also give them treats if you like, these are usually compressed hay with additions, but the pigs love the sound of rustling bags, and they seem to like treats too. It also doesnt hurt that guinea pigs eat people food, so you can use food stamps to buy food for your pigs. Welfare pigs.

health

A healthy life-span for a piggy is about 6 years. they can certainly go much longer than that--10 ish--but thats with a bit of luck and a lot of care. Before getting a piggy its important to have plans and money for their health needs. Guinea pigs require special vets who work on "exotic" animals like rabbits and birds. Because they are classified as exotic pets the vet bills tend to be higher for even basic checkups. Pigs adopted from big pet stores also frequently come with issues from the breeder. My pigs had mites, and so I had to buy ivermectin (that horse de-wormer that all the zoomers were eating during the pandemic) and apply a tiny amount to the backs of my pigs ears to rid them of the mites. Worse yet sometimes the pet store can misgender your pig and not even get cancelled; this generally leaves you with way more pigs than you intended on buying and is a big reason for the existence of guinea pig rescues. Before you bring a pig into your home make sure that you can provide the proper space and the proper food, so you dont have to worry as much about the health care costs.
